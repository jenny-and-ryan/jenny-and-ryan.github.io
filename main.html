If you're not local, don't worry!

By Air

The Pittsburgh "International" Airport (yeah, we go to Canada. And maybe Paris?) is about twenty minutes outside the city. You can catch an Uber / Lyft / cab to the hotel. The 28X bus also stops at the aiport and drops off Downtown and in Oakland.

By Train

Our Amtrak station is located downtown and connects to the Pennsylvanian line (Philadelphia, NYC) and the Capital Limited line (Chicago, DC). It's very relaxing.

By Car

Well, we're not sure how helpful we can be here, but if you do drive, make sure you head through the Fort Pitt Tunnel. It's the best intro to the city.

Staying in PGH

We've reserved a block of rooms at Such & Such Hotel, right around the corner from The Union Project. Ace Hotel? Hotel Indigo? SpringHill Suites? Airbnb is always a good option, too.

Here's some of our favorite things to do in the city. Pittsburgh has some excellent food trucks, and they often team up with the breweries listed below.

Foods

  • D's Six Pack and Dogz hot dogs and a good selection of beer
  • Frankuary More hot dogs & sausages, and tasty cocktails
  • Noodlehead best thai street food in Pittsburgh
  • Nicky's Thai Kitchen delicious everything-else-thai
  • Udipi Cafe best indian food in pittsburgh, location is kind of a dive and out of the way but the food is so good
  • Bakersfield real good tacos
  • Pamela's Diner greesy spoon breakfast, particularly excellent pancakes and potatoes
  • PGH Taco Truck yummy tacos, sometimes found at Brew Gentlemen
  • Mac & Gold Truck gourmet mac 'n cheese, sometimes found at Grist House

Drinks

(Fun Fact: most pubs and breweries in Pittsburgh are super kid-friendly.)

  • Independent Brewery our local spot; good selection of local and western PA beer
  • Brew Gentlemen started by a few CMU alumni; tasty, smooth beverages
  • Grist House great outdoor porch atmosphere; good booze, too
  • Maggie's Farm Rum Distillery really awesome cocktails in a cozy spot

Places

  • Carnegie Museum of Natural History lots of dinosaurs
  • The Warhol a chronological look through Andy Warhol's life
  • The Children's Museum fantastic, thoughtful museum for kids (and adults!)
  • The National Aviary there's a sloth. and a baby sloth! (also penguins and eagles and other awesome birds)
  • The Mattress Factory contemporary art museum with room-sized exhibits. it's a little weird
  • Frick Park good hiking and bike trails and a new environmental center that's super cool
